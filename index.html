Safety for One is Safety for All

The Do's.

Here we've got a list of things that we should do!

Wash Your Hands

Remember to wash your hand regularly. You can use either soap and water or an alcohol based sanitizer.

Cover While Sneezing

Always cover your nose and mouth while coughing and sneezing. Try to use your sleeve or your collar to sneeze into rather than using your hands, as shown in the picture.

See A Doctor

Don't keep your problems to yourself. Go see a doctor if you feel unwell. While visiting the doctor, be sure to wear a mask or use a cloth to cover your nose and mouth.

Throw Waste in Bins

Always discard used masks, tissues and all kinds of waste in general in bins or spaces alloted for waste disposal only. Let's make this a clean lockdown!

Call for Help

Having COVID-19 doesn't mean the end of the world. Our healthcare facilities are for you and you alone. Feel free to contact the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare or call the State Helpline number if you need help.

Cooperate With the Government

The government of every state of our country is struggling to keep up with the problems that come with COVID-19. Let's do our part by obeying what orders they give us, so that we can overcome this virus as fast as possible.

The Don'ts.

Here's a list of things that we should not do!

Don't Visit Crowded Areas

Try to stay away from busy places. Stay away from people with cough, sneezing, or fever.

Don't Touch Your Face

Try to avoid touching you eyes, nose and mouth regularly. Wash your hands before touching your face as much as possible.

Don't Cough without Covering

Always keep a handkerchief or cloth with you to cover your mouth when you sneeze or cough. Never spit in public either.

Don't Travel Unnecessarily

Do not travel unless it is absolutely neccessary. In case you do have to travel, avoid public transport as far as possible. Minimise the number of passengers in yoru vehicles (3 for Four-Wheelers and 2 for Two-Wheelers)

Don't Go out without Masks

Always wear your mask when you go outside. It will keep you, your family and the society safe.

Don't go into Crowds

The Corona Virus is more likely to spread in large gathers of people. Avoid such gatherings to keep yourself and your family safe. Also, feel free to report such gatherings to the local police stations as they are dangerous.
